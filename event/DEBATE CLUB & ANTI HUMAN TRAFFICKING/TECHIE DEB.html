Introduction

The debate is indispensable characteristics of an individual that makes them put forward their perspective in analyzing a situation of social cause linked with technology, whether it is in favour or against it. Techie is a debating event

Rules and Regulations

NUMBER OF ROUNDS: 3
Day 1 Prelims:
  • Each speaker will be given 3 minutes to speak and 2 minutes for the rebuttal.

    • Day 2 Semi-finals:
  • Semi-final Round: Eight students will qualify for this round, and each speaker will be given 5 minutes to speak and 3 minutes for the rebuttal.

    • Day2 FINALS:
  • Two students will qualify for this round, and each speaker will be given 6 minutes to speak and 4 minutes for the rebuttal. SELECTION OF WINNER: Based upon the discretion of Judges of each round. Top 4 for the Semi-finals and Top 2 for the Finals. Judges decision will be final.


  • ELIGIBILITY: All students studying in any Undergraduate Program are eligible to register

  • The debate is based on the amended version of Lincoln- Douglas format, i.e., one to one.

  • Debating ~ There are only individual registrations and hence no restriction on the number of registrations from a college.
  • The mode of competition is online.

  • Each zoom classroom would consist of 1 Judge, 2 Participants, 2 students, Coordinators.

  • As it is one to one debate, one team will speak FOR the motion, another one will speak AGAINST the motion.

  • All the participants are requested to prepare for both the proposition as well as the opposition where the motion that is to be presented by the participant will be announced by the judge in the zoom meeting.

  • Each participant will be allotted a student coordinator from the host institution to attend to the issues and doubts of the participants.

  • The organizing committee would provide the participants with the Zoom ID and Password, 1 hour prior to the respective rounds, through registered mail id and registered WhatsApp numbers.

  • Proper internet connection is required for participation in this competition, and in case of a blackout or improper connection, the participant may be disqualified by the organizing committee.

  • The official ID card of the participant must be sent to the student coordinator allotted.

  • All Participants must be in Proper formal attire during the competition.

  • Participants are not allowed to interrupt the fellow participant when he or she is presenting.

  • Participants are requested to be mindful of the time limit. Exceeding the time limit would result in negative marking.

  • No participant will be allowed to argue with the judge, and the judgeÂ’s decision will be conclusive.

  • Participants must refrain from using obscene words and are requested to maintain the dignity and decorum of the competition.

  • Organizers reserve the right to alter/change the rules/ or procedure of the competition if the need so arises.
